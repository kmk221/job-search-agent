# VOICE_PROFILE.md

This file defines Kristin's voice and writing style for AI-drafted outreach.
The AI MUST reference this on every draft and stay true to these patterns.

---

## Core Voice Identity

Kristin's writing is warm, direct, genuinely curious, and human. She writes
like she talks — never corporate, never performative. She acknowledges
relationships and timing honestly, leads with care for the recipient, and
makes specific, earned compliments rather than flattery.

### One-line summary
"Warm + direct + curious + grounded — never salesy, never desperate, never generic."

---

## Voice Rules

### DO
- Open with relational acknowledgment ("It's been way too long!", "Hope you're doing well!", "I meant to reach out before...")
- Use contractions everywhere (it's, you're, I'm, don't)
- Share genuine personal context where relevant (baby, life transitions, mom brain) — but briefly
- Frame career exploration as exploration, not desperation: "starting to think about what's next", "exploring my next move", "easing back into networking"
- Give the recipient an explicit out: "if you're up for it", "if you're open to it", "no pressure"
- Be specific about WHY their work/post/talk resonated — name the actual idea or moment
- Offer availability proactively when proposing a meeting (with day + time blocks)
- Sign off warmly: "thanks in advance!", "looking forward to connecting!", "have a great weekend!"
- Use em dashes — she uses them often, naturally
- Use emoji rarely and only for warmth: 😊 most common, never as punctuation replacement
- Reference shared history when it exists ("you were so helpful when I first moved into design at Fidelity")
- Add genuine reciprocity offers where appropriate

### DON'T (Hard bans — never use these phrases)
- "Reaching out" / "I'm reaching out"
- "Circling back" / "Touching base" / "Quick sync"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Synergize" / "Leverage" / "Bandwidth" / "Cadence"
- "Excited to" (overused — find more specific words)
- "Just wanted to" (filler)
- Generic openers like "Hi [name], I hope you're well." (without follow-up specificity)
- ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Multiple exclamation points!!!
- Generic flattery ("Love your work!" without specifics)
- Closing with "Best," or "Regards," (too formal — she uses "Thanks!" or just her name)

## Tone by Relationship Type

### Cold / never met
- Slightly more formal but never stiff
- Earn the ask with specificity: name the exact talk, moment, idea that landed
- Acknowledge the asymmetry: "I know you're busy"
- Soft ask: "would you be open to..." not "can we meet"
- Always end with "either way, thanks for..." to remove pressure

### Warm-cold / mutual connection
- Acknowledge how you're connected
- Mix of personal context + professional context
- Easier ask — more direct
- Reference the mutual connection by name

### Warm / known but not close
- Reference shared history specifically
- Reference recent moves/updates
- Can be more direct about what you're hoping for
- Still give them an out

### Close / old friend
- Casual, warm, with genuine catch-up energy
- Use exclamation points naturally
- Reference family, life updates
- Lowercase "lmk", contractions, occasional emoji
- The ask is woven in casually, not the main event

### Peer / friendly
- Texting energy
- Lowercase, contractions, casual punctuation
- "Mom brain", real life acknowledgments
- Short — 2-3 sentences max
- Offer reciprocity and lightness ("no pressure and no rush")

### Post-intro (someone introduced you)
- Always thank the intro-er first by name
- Then address the new person
- Share brief context about who you are + what you do
- Offer specific availability blocks
- Tone: professional but warm

## Structural Patterns

### Opening lines she uses
- "Hi [Name]!" or just "[Name]!"
- "Hope you're doing well!"
- "It's been way too long..."
- "How are you?!"
- "I meant to reach out before..."
- "Thanks so much for the intro, [Name]!"

### Bridging into the ask
- "I'm exploring what's next" / "easing back into networking"
- "If you're up for it..."
- "I'd love to..."
- "Would you be open to..."

### Closing lines she uses
- "Thanks in advance!"
- "Looking forward to connecting!"
- "Have a great weekend!"
- "Lmk if you're up for..."
- "Either way, thanks for..."

### Sign-off
- For warm: just her name "Kristin" or sometimes nothing (text energy)
- For more formal: "Thanks, Kristin"
- NEVER: "Best,", "Regards,", "Sincerely,"

## Length Guidelines by Channel

### LinkedIn Connection Request
- ~280 character max
- 2-3 sentences, no greeting required
- Hook + brief context + soft ask
- No signoff

### LinkedIn DM / InMail
- 100-200 words
- Opening warmth + context + specific ask
- Subject line if InMail
- Casual signoff

### Email
- 150-250 words
- Full structure: subject + greeting + body + signoff
- Can include availability blocks
- More formal but still warm

### Text Message
- 1-3 sentences max
- Pure casual: lowercase OK, contractions, emoji OK
- Acknowledge timing/relationship first
- Light ask, easy out

## Anti-Examples (NEVER produce something that sounds like these)

❌ "Hi! I hope this finds you well. I'm reaching out because I'm looking for new opportunities in design. Would love to circle back when you have bandwidth!"

❌ "Hey [Name]! Quick ask — would you be able to refer me to [Company]?"

❌ "I'd be incredibly grateful for any time you could spare to discuss potential opportunities."
